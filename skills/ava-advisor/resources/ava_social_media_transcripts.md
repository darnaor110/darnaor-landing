## Video 1 — איך ליצור ריל מעולה (How to Create a Killer Short-Form Video)

**Summary:** A complete framework for creating viral short-form content, broken into 7 key factors: topic, hooks (verbal/written/visual), value, script angle, CTA, format, and editing style — with step-by-step guidance on each.

---

### Background

After studying 7,356+ viral short-form videos and taking 117 pages of notes, the presenter breaks down the exact process for going from blank page to a fully optimized short-form video.

---

### The 7 Factors of Every Viral Short

Every viral short consists of seven key factors:

1. **Topic** — What the video is about
2. **Hooks** — Verbal (what you hear), Written (what you read), Visual (what you see) — all in the first 3 seconds
3. **Value** — The meat of the video; the educational takeaway
4. **Script Angle** — The structural direction the video takes
5. **CTA** — Call to action at the end
6. **Format** — The engaging way the video is filmed
7. **Editing Style** — Text, colors, visuals, and music

---

### Factor 1 — Topic

- Write out your main broad niche (fitness, nutrition, business, sales, fashion, etc.)
- Then write 5–7 subniches under that niche
  - Example for fitness: lifting, cardio, nutrition, protein, sleep, getting a six-pack
- Choose one subniche as the topic of your short

---

### Factor 2 — Hooks (Verbal, Written, Visual)

**Core Principle:** Never throw spaghetti at the wall. Research proven outlier hooks.

**What is an outlier hook?**
- Videos from small to mid-size creators that drastically outperform their average
- The 5x Rule: the video has 5x the views compared to that creator's follower count
- If it went viral once, it has a strong chance of going viral again

**3 Research Methods (all done on Instagram):**

**Method 1 — Creator Research:**
- Go to the Instagram Explore page
- Search your topic → click Accounts or Reels tab
- Find creators in your niche → find their top 5–10 most viral videos
- Use the **Sortfeed Chrome extension** to sort by views and export a CSV
- Save videos that follow the 5x rule

**Method 2 — Keyword Research:**
- Go to Explore → search bar → type your video topic
- Click the Reels tab → find and save videos that follow the 5x rule
- Try multiple variations of your topic keyword

**Method 3 — Reels Recommendations Tab (Intentional Scrolling):**
- After doing methods 1 and 2, check your Reels recommendation tab
- The algorithm now feeds you niche-relevant content
- Scroll and save hidden outlier gems that wouldn't appear in keyword or creator research

**Goal:** Find a verbal, written, and visual hook that each follow the 5x rule.

---

### Factor 3 — Value

**The meat of the video — the educational takeaway.**

Value comes from two sources:
- **Your own knowledge/experience** (recommended if you're a true expert)
- **An external source** (study, article, expert) — always give credit if used

**Fluffy value vs. Measurable value:**
- Fluffy: unclear, not specific, people don't know what to do with it
- Measurable: specific, actionable, and clear — always use this

> If you're truly an expert in your niche, use your own knowledge. It keeps you authentic and authoritative. Avoid posting about things you don't know well.

---

### Factor 4 — Script Angle

One video topic can become 7 different videos using different angles:

1. **Tutorial** — Step-by-step process, framework, or acronym
2. **Comparison** — Compare different actions, methods, or products
3. **Mythbust / Common Mistake** — Break a common belief or debunk a mistake
4. **Do's vs. Don'ts / Right vs. Wrong** — What not to do vs. what to do
5. **Educational Tip/Hack** — A single niche-related tip or lesson
6. **Transformation** — Personal or client before/after result
7. **Challenge** — Attempting to achieve a specific result

Each angle can be delivered as an **educational reel** or a **storytelling** approach.

---

### Factor 5 — CTA (Call to Action)

**Two main goals:**
- Build your following / personal brand → use a Follow CTA
- Drive leads to a freebie, product, or service → use ManyChat

**ManyChat Setup (for Instagram):**
1. Click New Automation → Plus New Trigger
2. Select: User comments on your post or video
3. Choose: all videos or a specific video → Continue
4. Set your trigger word (under 5–7 letters) → Continue
5. Type the message you want to send → Plus New Button
6. Add your link (freebie, product, or service page)
7. Click Set Live

---

### Factor 6 — Video Format

Sitting down and talking is boring. Use an engaging format to hold attention:

1. **Talking back and forth** — Smart vs. dumb character teaching a concept
2. **Visual format** — Props/visuals used to explain a concept
3. **Voiceover** — Video/images on screen with narration over them
4. **Multitasking** — Teaching while doing something unrelated (cooking, cleaning, makeup)
5. **Setting change** — Changing locations throughout the video
6. **Shot/angle change** — New shot or angle every 2 seconds
7. **Clone format** — Doubling yourself on screen to compare two things
8. **Whiteboard** — Using a whiteboard to explain a concept
9. **Q&A** — One or multiple people being asked a question on camera
10. **Green screen** — Filming with relevant videos/images playing behind you
11. **Reaction** — Reacting to a mega-viral video in your niche, then adding your expert take

---

### Factor 7 — Editing Style

**Colors:** Choose 2–3 main brand colors.

**Fonts:** Choose 2–3:
- Primary font — for written hooks and big bold text
- Secondary font — for regular captions
- Accent font (optional) — for annotations, used sparingly

**Visuals:** Choose 2–3 consistent visual types:
- Screenshots, screen recordings, B-roll, images, charts/graphs, etc.

> Choose these once, then edit all your videos consistently — this is how people recognize your style.

---

### Final Notes

- You can never guarantee results on social media, but you can optimize for a higher chance of success
- Some creators get their first big win within a week; others take months — the only failure is stopping
- Do not sacrifice integrity for views. Trends and fluffy content may get short-term wins but attract followers who don't care about you
- Post content you're proud of — educational, vulnerable, personal, raw, real — that's what builds a real personal brand and generates leads

## Video 2 — איך לגדול באינסטגרם ב-2026 (How to Grow on Instagram in 2026)

**Summary:** A stage-by-stage growth roadmap from 0 to 100K followers on Instagram, broken into three levels with specific steps, strategies, and content frameworks for each phase.

---

### Overview

Three growth stages:
- **Level 1:** 0 → 1,000 followers
- **Level 2:** 1,000 → 10,000 followers
- **Level 3:** 10,000 → 100,000 followers

---

### Level 1 — 0 to 1,000 Followers

This is the hardest stage. Most people fail here. Follow these steps:

**Step 1 — Personal Brand Basics ("Building the House" Analogy)**

*The Foundation:*
- Define your main broad niche (Fitness, Business, Nutrition, Sales, Beauty, Fashion, etc.)
- Write 5–7 subniches under that niche

*The Interior (the "personal" in personal brand):*
- **Educational content:** Your own knowledge and experience
- **Storytelling content:** Personal and business stories — wins, losses, founder story
- **Authority content:** Your personal transformation or client transformations you helped engineer

*The Exterior (what everyone sees):*
- **Colors:** Choose 2–3 brand colors
- **Fonts:** Primary (for hooks/bold text), Secondary (for captions), Accent (optional, for annotations)
- **Visuals:** 2–3 consistent visual types (screenshots, B-roll, charts, etc.)

**Step 2 — Optimize Your Profile**

| Element | Wrong | Right |
|---|---|---|
| Link in bio | Linktree with multiple links | One link to a landing/freebie page |
| Username | Random or confusing | Your full name or business name |
| Tagline | Just your name | Name + separator + niche keyword |
| Profile picture | Far away, multiple people, busy background | Close-up, solid background, crystal clear |
| Bio | Messy, unclear | 4-line framework: who you are + what you do |

Use **PFP Maker** to adjust profile picture to your brand colors.

**Step 3 — Posting Amount & Schedule**
- Choose a level: Easy / Moderate / Hard
- Pick specific days to stay accountable
- The exact days don't matter — consistency does

**Step 4 — Create Short-Form Content Scripts**

Every short has 7 factors (see Video 1 for full breakdown):

1. **Topic** — Choose one subniche
2. **Hooks** — Research proven viral outlier hooks (5x rule); find using Creator Research, Keyword Research, or Reels Recommendations Tab; use the **Sortfeed Chrome extension**
3. **Value** — Use your own knowledge (recommended) or a credible source; always use measurable value
4. **Script Angle** — Tutorial / Comparison / Mythbust / Do's vs Don'ts / Tip-Hack / Transformation / Challenge; choose educational or storytelling approach
5. **CTA** — Follow CTA or ManyChat CTA to freebie/landing page
6. **Format** — Choose an engaging filming format (back-and-forth, visual, voiceover, multitasking, setting change, shot change, clone, whiteboard, Q&A, green screen, reaction)
7. **Editing Style** — Use the colors, fonts, and visuals you defined in Step 1

Batch 1 week to 1 month of scripts at a time.

**Step 5 — Film Your Shorts**
- Script format: black text for script, red for shot/action notes, green for editing notes
- Equipment: phone + Amazon tripod + Amazon microphone
- Film one bullet at a time
- Create one folder per batch, sub-folders per video — upload clips immediately after filming

**Step 6 — Edit**
- Use CapCut yourself, or hire an editor (recommended for business owners)

**Step 7 — Upload**
- Caption structure:
  - Line 1: The written hook of the video
  - (Double space)
  - Line 2: The CTA
  - (Double space)
  - Line 3: 3–5 hashtags (niche, target audience, subtopic)
  - Note: Hashtags don't boost performance much, but keywords help reach the right people

**Step 8 — Repeat**
- Keep batching and posting until you hit 1K
- Average: 50–100 videos (takes longer for brand new accounts)

---

### Level 2 — 1,000 to 10,000 Followers

**Step 1 — Study**
- Find your top 5 best-performing videos and 5 worst-performing videos
- Use the **Edits app** (by Instagram) for better analytics

**Step 2 — Double Down (Templatizing Your Viral Outliers)**

1. Find your outliers: videos with 5x your average views
2. Analyze: break down all 7 components of that viral video
3. Double down — use components from it in new videos:
   - Keep most components the same, just change the value slightly
   - Keep the format/structure, change everything else
   - Keep everything the same, just change the filming format *(this strategy got 12M views and 83K followers in 90 days)*

**Step 3 — The 70/20/10 Rule** (created by Caleb)

When batching a month of content:
- **70%** — What you know works (proven outlier hooks/topics from your niche)
- **20%** — Double downs on your own best-performing videos
- **10%** — Experiments with new ideas you create from scratch

**Step 4 — Continue**
- Repeat Steps 4–7 from Level 1 using the 70/20/10 rule
- Keep going until you hit 10K

---

### Level 3 — 10,000 to 100,000 Followers

**Step 1** — Continue with Steps 4–7 from Level 1, still using the 70/20/10 framework monthly.

**Key distinction: Following vs. Personal Brand**

| | Creator 1 (Following) | Creator 2 (Personal Brand) |
|---|---|---|
| Results | Fluctuating | Consistent |
| Content | Trends, aesthetic B-roll, one content type | Mix of all 3 types below |
| Problem | No connection — people don't care about the creator | Builds community that cares about you |

**Three content types for building a personal brand:**
1. **Educational** — Real, deep, measurable, actionable value
2. **Storytelling** — Vulnerable, raw, real content about your business and yourself
3. **Authority** — Your personal or client results and transformations

These three combined create the trifecta: a community that knows, likes, and trusts you.

**Final Advice**

Do not post anything you'll regret (inauthentic content, "icky" content) just to go viral. Stay true to yourself.

## Video 3 — איך ליצור סרטון סטוריטלינג ויראלי (How to Create Viral Storytelling Short-Form Content)

**Summary:** A step-by-step guide for creating viral storytelling short-form videos, covering the 7 story types, how to find and build script structures, how to write and plan your video, and how to film, edit, and upload it.

---

### Why Storytelling Content?

After studying 2,756+ viral storytelling videos and creating 100+ for clients and her own brand, the presenter argues that storytelling content is now *required* to build a personal brand and convert followers into leads — not just a nice-to-have.

---

### Step 1 — Choose Your Story Type

There are 7 main story types:

1. **My Story / Founder Story** — How you started your business or life story
   > *"I started my pajama business when I was 19 with $800..."*

2. **Win Story** — A big or small achievement, milestone, or goal you hit
   > *"Six months ago, my wife and I dropped out of school, got married, and decided to chase our dreams..."*

3. **Loss Story** — A mistake, failure, or bad result in your life or business
   > *"Last year, I sold my business for 52 million. Here's every idea I tried before my big win..."*

4. **Lesson Story** — What you learned from a big win or loss
   > *"Nothing could have prepared me for how it feels to be in your 20s with your friends scattered all over the country..."*

5. **Transformation Story** — Personal before/after or client before/after
   > *"10 years it took me from living in a car to living in this penthouse..."*

6. **Challenge Story** — A challenge you set for yourself and your journey completing it
   > *"Is it possible to get an internship without your daddy being the CEO of Apple?"*

7. **Big Goal / Dream Journey** — Announcing your big dream and the plan to achieve it
   > *"It's been a dream of mine to be an author since I was 17..."*

---

### Step 2 — Choose a Script Structure

All great movies, books, and TV episodes follow a storytelling structure — short-form content is no different.

**How to create your own script structure:**

1. Find a storytelling video matching the story type you want to create (must follow the 5x rule)
2. Transcribe the video: click the 3 dots → Copy Link → go to **gettranscribe.ai** → paste link → Get Transcript
3. Templatize using ChatGPT with this prompt:
   > *"This is a transcript from a viral video. Please make it into a script template that could be used for any niche. Keep the overall format/structure of the video and just make it a fill-in-the-blank template version."*
4. Finalize: add to a Google Doc, make adjustments, and keep a running bank of these structures

---

### Step 3 — Write Your Script

Use your chosen script structure/template and write your script line by line in bullet points.

**Example script** (fictional fitness coach, Katniss Everdine):

> Is it possible to lose 50 lbs while being a broke, busy college kid in 6 months after being "fat" your entire life? Well, 5 years ago, I decided to try it — and here's what happened.
>
> I created a three-step game plan:
> 1. I found my maintenance calories using a TDEE calculator, then subtracted 500 calories to lose 1 lb per week
> 2. I built a meal plan hitting 100g protein/day while staying under my calorie limit
> 3. I created a workout plan: lifting 5x per week + 10,000 steps daily
>
> 6 months later, I lost 50 lbs — and this experience led me to start Flex, to help women just like me do the same.

**For hooks in storytelling videos:**
- Find viral 5x-rule hooks from small/mid-size creators
- Example used above: the "Is it possible?" hook structure — already proven viral across many niches

---

### Step 4 — Choose a Format

Three formats that work especially well for storytelling videos:

1. **Voiceover** — Video/images on screen with narration playing over them
2. **Shot/Angle Change** — New shot or angle every 2 seconds
3. **Audio B-roll + Text** — A new image or background video per line of the story every 1–3 seconds, with an emotional soundtrack

---

### Step 5 — Plan Your Visuals and Shots

**Script planning format:**
- Write each line of script as a bullet
- **Red text bullet** under each line = shot you need to film
- **Green text bullet** under each line = editing notes (past photos, videos, or other media to add)

Use past photos and videos from the time period you're talking about — they make storytelling videos significantly more powerful.

---

### Step 6 — Film, Edit, and Upload

**Filming:**
- Film the voiceover + any red (shot) bullets from the script
- Equipment: iPhone + Amazon tripod + Amazon mic

**Editing:**
- Hand the script (with all red/green notes) + all filmed clips to an editor
- Recommend adding an emotional background song — it significantly affects how the video feels

**Uploading (Caption Framework):**
- Line 1: The hook of the video
- (Double space)
- Line 2: The CTA
- (Double space)
- Line 3 (optional): 3–5 hashtags — target audience, topic, niche

---

### Final Thought

Not every storytelling video will hit, but they tend to outperform because they're more relatable. The content landscape is shifting — educational content alone is no longer enough. Storytelling is what builds a community, not just a following, and it converts far better for both followers and leads.

## Video 4 — איך לעשות הוק מעולה (How to Create a Killer Hook)

**Summary:** A deep-dive into the three types of hooks, how to find proven viral hooks using a systematic research process, the 7 hook angles, how to build hook stacks, and cross-niche hook templates.

---

### Why Hooks Matter

The first 3 seconds of your video determine the outcome of the entire reel. Top creators know how to create a perfect hook repeatedly.

---

### The 3 Types of Hooks

The more hooks you use together, the higher the chance of success:

- **Verbal Hook** — What viewers *hear* in the first 3 seconds
- **Written Hook** — What viewers *read* in the first 3 seconds
- **Visual Hook** — What viewers *see* in the first 3 seconds

---

### Viral Outlier Hooks — The 5x Rule

Never throw spaghetti at the wall. Always research proven viral hooks.

- **Definition:** Videos from small to mid-size creators that drastically outperform their average content
- **The 5x Rule:** The video has 5x views compared to the creator's follower count (organic views, not paid)
- **Logic:** If the hook already went viral once, it has a strong chance of going viral again

---

### Research Methods — Finding Viral Hooks (Monthly Process)

**Step 1 — Create Keywords**

Build a list of research keywords based on:
1. Your niche (broad industry)
2. Your subtopics (subniches)
3. Your occupation/professional title

**Step 2 — Reset the Algorithm (Research Account)**

- Create a separate "dummy" Instagram account for research only
- Reset its algorithm: Profile Settings → Content Preferences → Reset Suggested Content

**Step 3 — Keyword Research**

1. Open Instagram on the dummy account
2. Go to Explore → search bar
3. Search your niche, subtopic, or keyword
4. Scroll and save hooks that follow the 5x rule
5. Repeat for every keyword on your list

**Step 4 — Account Research**

*Part A — Find Accounts:*
- After keyword search, click "Accounts" tab — analyze and screenshot accounts you like
- From a creator's account, click "Follow" and browse suggested accounts for more creators
- Check who those creators follow for even more niche accounts

*Part B — Find Outlier Hooks from Saved Accounts:*
1. Go to each saved account
2. Click the Reels icon on their profile
3. Click the dropdown → select **Most Viewed**
4. Save the viral hooks you like that follow the 5x rule
5. Repeat for every saved account

**Step 5 — Reels Tab Research (Hidden Gems)**

After completing all keyword and account research:
1. Open the dummy Instagram account
2. Click the Reels tab at the bottom
3. Scroll and save niche hooks that follow the 5x rule

*Note: Because you've been feeding the algorithm your specific niche, the recommendations will now be highly targeted — this is how you find the hidden gems that keyword/creator research wouldn't surface.*

---

### The 7 Hook Angles

Most viral hooks fall into one of these categories:

1. **Tutorial** — Step-by-step process, framework, or acronym
2. **Comparison** — Comparing different actions or methods to show what works better
3. **Mythbust / Common Mistake** — Debunking a myth or common error in your niche
4. **Do's vs. Don'ts** — Showing what's right vs. wrong in your niche
5. **Educational Tip / Hack** — A single niche lesson, tip, or hack
6. **Transformation** — A before/after result
7. **Challenge** — Creating or participating in a niche-related challenge

---

### Hook Stacks

A **Hook Stack** = combining a verbal + written + visual hook for one video.

Create a hook stack for every video you plan.

**Example Hook Stacks (styling niche):**

*Example 1:*
- Written Hook: *"Hairstyle neckline guide"*
- Verbal Hook: *"This is the best hairstyle for different tops"*
- Visual Hook: Clone of yourself

*Example 2:*
- Written Hook: *"How to dress for your proportions"*
- Verbal Hook: *"How to dress for your body type"*
- Visual Hook: Drawn visuals and labels of different body types on screen

---

### Cross-Niche Hook Templates

If you can't find a niche-specific hook, or you spot a successful hook in another niche, you can templatize it for your own.

**Popular viral templates:**
- *"Is it possible...?"*
- *"600 days ago / a year ago / 6 months ago..."*
- *"Did you know if you / Did you know that..."*
- *"Three levels of [topic]..."*
- *"[Smart Guy/Couple] vs. [Dumb Guy/Couple] when it comes to [topic]..."*

Use niche-specific hooks first — these templates are a strategic bonus option.

---

### Final Note

One single video can completely change your channel. Building hooks strategically gives you the best possible shot at a big win.

## Video 5 — איך יוצרים קטנים גדלו מהר באינסטגרם (How Small Creators Grew Fast on Instagram)

**Summary:** Based on studying the fastest-growing Instagram creators, this video identifies three repeatable growth strategies: series content, posting a mix of three content types, and doubling down on your own viral outliers.

---

### Introduction

After studying the fastest-growing creators' accounts and taking 37 pages of notes, three repeatable patterns and frameworks emerged that any creator can use to grow from zero to hundreds of thousands of followers.

---

### Part 1 — Series Content

**What is Series Content?**
A planned sequence of related posts that follow the same theme, format, or promise — released over time to keep people coming back for the next episode.

**Why it works:**
People follow to see the next part, especially if you end on a cliffhanger. This is Netflix's business model applied to short-form content.

**How to Create Your Own Series:**

**Step 1 — Pick a Niche/Topic**
Choose an overall theme for your series.

**Step 2 — Pick a Series Type**

1. **The Daily Series**
   - Post a video each day
   - State the day number and restate the series goal in the hook
   - Give a small update on a win, problem, or progress from that day
   - Ends when the goal is completed (can run very long — e.g., Day 239)

2. **The Progress Update Series**
   - Based around completing one single goal
   - Each episode = a progress update (not necessarily daily)
   - Can be structured as step-by-step completion of a plan
   - Example: Haley's series on each step of writing her book to become a NYT bestselling author

3. **The Lesson Series**
   - Each episode = a new lesson learned or educational take on a topic
   - Examples: Khloe's "30 Lessons by 30" series, Ken's "Raw Faith" series

**Step 3 — Create a Rough Draft**
- Plan the number of episodes
- Outline the premise of each episode
- Set a posting schedule for accountability

**Step 4 — Script, Film, Edit, and Upload**

Best formats for series content:
- **Shot/Angle Change** — New shot or angle every 2 seconds
- **Visual Format** — Props/visuals to demonstrate a concept
- **Voiceover Format**
- **Setting Change** — Film in a new location every 1–2 lines

---

### Part 2 — Three Content Types

For creators who don't want to do series content — post a mix of all three:

1. **Educational Content** — Niche lessons, tips, mistakes, step-by-step tutorials → builds authority
2. **Personal / Storytelling Content** — Background, founder story, wins/losses, lifestyle content → builds connection and community
3. **Authority Content** — Personal or client results/testimonials → builds trust and generates leads

> Note: Authority content typically gets the fewest views — that's normal.

Posting all three = personal brand (consistent views) vs. just a following (fluctuating views).

---

### Part 3 — Doubling Down (Templatizing Viral Outliers)

Smart creators recreate their viral videos over and over again with small tweaks each time.

**Step 1 — Find Your Own Outliers**
Videos that drastically outperformed your average (e.g., 10K+ views when your average is 1K).

**Step 2 — Analyze**
Break down all 7 components of that viral video (topic, hooks, value, script angle, CTA, format, editing style).

**Step 3 — Double Down**

Three ways to do it:
- **Example A:** Keep most components the same, change the value slightly
- **Example B:** Keep the format/structure, adjust most other components
- **Example C:** Keep all components the same, only change the filming format *(a 90-day test of this method resulted in 12M+ views)*

---

### Final Note on Growth

Creators who explode didn't grow overnight. Some took 2–4 years of consistent posting before they blew up. The key is that none of them gave up. Growing a new account is the hardest part — but as long as you keep posting, you have not failed.

## Video 6 — איך ליצור תסריט מעולה (How to Create a Killer Script)

**Summary:** Explains the concept of viral script structures — fill-in-the-blank templates that go viral across any niche — and gives a step-by-step process for finding, transcribing, templatizing, and using them in your own content.

---

### The Core Insight

Did you notice that this fitness video and this business video use the exact same script structure?

> *"Fried eggs are bad for your weight loss process. Scrambled eggs are good, but boiled eggs are excellent."*
> *"Posting randomly is bad for growth. Posting four times is good, and posting seven times is excellent."*

Just like there are fill-in-the-blank **hook structures**, there are also fill-in-the-blank **script structures** that go viral across any niche.

---

### What is a Script Structure?

A script structure is a fill-in-the-blank template for your entire script — not just the hook.

**Examples by type:**

*Easy Script Structures:*
- *"If you drink apple cider vinegar with lemon, your belly feels flatter and less puffy..."*
- *"If you take creatine, it will increase your strength in the gym..."*

*Complex Script Structures:*
- *"Did you know that if you twist a potato on this part of the grater?"*
- *"Did you know if you combine some rolled oats and nut butter..."*

*Educational Script Structures:*
- *"Three levels of cookies..."*
- *"Three levels of tomato pasta..."*

*Storytelling Script Structures:*
- *"Is it possible to love God and quit swearing in 10 days, even though you're used to cussing around all your friends?"*
- *"Is it possible to write a successful college essay for Harvard in just 1 hour?"*

> **Key insight:** All trends on social media are actually just repeatable script structures.

---

### How to Find and Create Script Structures

**Step 1 — Find an Outlier Video**
Find a video with a script structure you like that follows the **5x Rule** (5x views vs. the creator's following).

**Step 2 — Transcribe the Video**
- Click the 3 dots on the reel → Copy Link
- Go to **gettranscribe.ai** → paste the link → Get Transcript

**Step 3 — Templatize (Using AI)**
Open ChatGPT and use this prompt:
> *"This is a transcript from a viral video. Please make it into a script template that can be used for any niche. Keep the overall format/structure of the video and just make it a fill-in-the-blank version."*
Then paste in the transcript.

**Step 4 — Finalize**
- Add to a Google Doc, make any adjustments
- Build a running bank of educational and storytelling script structures to reuse

---

### Low Effort vs. High Effort Script Structures

**Easy Script Structures (Low Effort)**
- Easily recognizable, often repetitive (e.g., rating reels, "Wake up at 4am — 100")
- Great for **views and reach** — easy to understand
- Weak for **followers** — example: 1M views reel → only 200 followers

**Complex Script Structures (High Effort)**
- Harder to recognize, don't look templated
- Typically hyper-educational OR extremely personal/raw storytelling
- Examples:
  - *"Is it possible to love God and quit swearing in 10 days... Yes."*
  - *"Two couples are trying to invest. Two husbands take on grocery shopping. Two high school students trying to get into Ivy League..."*
- Fewer views sometimes, but **much better view-to-follower ratio**

> **Recommendation:** Lean toward complex, high-effort script structures — they build actual audiences, not just view counts.

---

### Final Thought

Find viral videos in any niche → templatize the script into a structure → fill it with your own information. Very few people do this — which is exactly why it works so well.

## Video 7 — איך להגדיל את האינסטגרם ל-100K בחצי שנה (0 to 100K Followers — The Cheat Codes)

**Summary:** Six "cheat codes" for growing from 0 to 100K followers faster, covering outlier research, hook stacks, posting three content types, series content, script structures, and doubling down on viral videos.

---

### Cheat Code 1 — Outliers

Never throw spaghetti at the wall. Always research proven viral outlier hooks and topics.

- **What is an Outlier?** Videos from small to mid-size creators that massively outperformed their average
- **The 5x Rule:** 5x views compared to the creator's follower count

**Research Methods:**

1. **Creator Research**
   - Instagram Explore → search niche/topic → click Accounts tab
   - Screenshot accounts you like
   - Use **Sortfeed Chrome extension**: go to creator's profile → Reels tab → click Sortfeed → All Items → Views → export CSV
   - Save viral hooks that follow the 5x rule

2. **Keyword Research**
   - Instagram Explore → search bar → type niche/keywords/topics
   - Scroll — Instagram recommends viral videos around that topic
   - Find and save outliers
   - Search multiple term variations for best coverage

3. **Reels Recommendation Tab (Hidden Gems)**
   - Complete methods 1 and 2 first
   - Check your Reels recommendation tab — the algorithm now shows targeted niche content
   - Save any hidden outliers you find here

---

### Cheat Code 2 — Hook Stacks

Three types of hooks: Verbal (what you hear) + Written (what you read) + Visual (what you see) in the first 3 seconds.

A **Hook Stack** = combining all three from proven outlier videos into one video.

> When you take proven viral hooks from three different outlier videos and stack them together, you dramatically increase your chances of a big win.

---

### Cheat Code 3 — Three Types of Videos

Never post just one type of content. Post all three to build a personal brand instead of just a following:

1. **Educational** — Niche lessons, tips, mistakes, tutorials → builds authority
2. **Personal / Storytelling** — Background, founder story, wins/losses, lifestyle → builds connection
3. **Authority** — Personal or client results/testimonials → builds trust and generates leads

---

### Cheat Code 4 — Series Content

A planned sequence of related posts released over time to keep people coming back.

**Series Types:**
- **Daily Series** — Post every day with a day count + progress update (highly effective but intensive)
- **Progress Update Series** — Based on one goal, episodic updates (less time-intensive)
- **Lesson Series** — Each episode = a new lesson or educational take

**Process:**
1. Pick a niche/topic
2. Choose a series type
3. Plan episodes and posting schedule
4. Script, film, edit, post using engaging formats (shot/angle change, visual, voiceover, setting change)

---

### Cheat Code 5 — Script Structures

Fill-in-the-blank script templates for the meat of your video.

**Process:**
1. Find an outlier video with a structure you like
2. Transcribe it using **gettranscribe.ai**
3. Templatize with ChatGPT: *"Make this into a fill-in-the-blank script template that works for any niche"*
4. Save in a Google Doc bank
5. Combine with your viral hook stack (Cheat Code 2)

> All trends on social media are actually just script structures.

---

### Cheat Code 6 — Doubling Down

Smart creators recreate their viral videos with small tweaks every single time.

**Process:**
1. Find your outliers (videos with 5x your average views)
2. Break down all 7 components
3. Recreate with one of three approaches:
   - **A:** Keep most components, slightly change the value
   - **B:** Keep format/structure, adjust other components
   - **C:** Keep all components the same, only change the filming format

> A 90-day experiment with this method resulted in 12M+ views and 83K followers.

## Video 8 — מה הסיסטם כדי להתפוצץ ברשתות החברתיות (The Content System for Exploding on Social Media)

### The Three Content Types

There are three main types of content to post on social media. Each serves a different purpose, and your goal determines how much of each you post.

---

### 1. TOFU — Top of Funnel

**Goal: Views**

Broad, easy-to-understand, educational content — or videos based on simple, repeatable script structures.

**Examples:**
- Ranking videos / tier lists (e.g., "Rich Dad Poor Dad tier list")
- Q&A type videos
- Easy repeatable script structures (e.g., *"If you drink apple cider vinegar with lemon..."*)
- Content that the average person could be interested in, not super in-depth

**Recommended CTAs:**
- Follow CTA
- Freebie CTA (high lead potential due to volume)

---

### 2. MOFU — Middle of Funnel

**Goal: Followers + Connection**

Personal, raw, real, getting-to-know-you content.

**Examples:**
- My story / founder story (e.g., *"Starting a business with $800"*)
- Product or service story
- Day in the life
- Personal or business challenge videos (e.g., *"Quitting swearing in 10 days"*)
- Wins, losses, and lessons
- Series content (e.g., *"9 weeks ago, me and my boy bought 2.5 acres of land..."*)

**Recommended CTAs:**
- Follow CTA

---

### 3. BOFU — Bottom of Funnel

**Goal: Leads + Trust**

In-depth educational content for people already in your niche.

**Examples:**
- Educational step-by-step tutorials (e.g., *"How to plan a full month of content in 45 minutes"*)
- One-off educational tips, hacks, comparisons
- Personal before/after transformations
- Client before/after transformations and testimonials
- Fake case study videos (e.g., *"If I had to create content for a brand like Gymshark, here's what I'd do..."*)
- Fake "what I would do in XYZ situation" videos (e.g., *"This is how I would invest $1,000 from scratch"*)

**Important:** Short-form content is NOT an ad. Post high-value educational content and attach a lead CTA with social proof at the end — don't just straight-up sell.

**Recommended CTAs:**
- CTA to your freebie
- CTA to your paid offer

---

| **10,000–1M** | 2/week (1x Follow, 1x Freebie) + 1 TOFU Double Down/week | 1/week (Follow CTA) | 3/week (1x Follow CTA, 2x Paid Offer CTA) |

## Video 11 — איך להעזר ב-AI ליצירת תוכן (How to Use AI for Content Creation)

**Summary:** A practical guide to implementing AI into a content workflow, covering tool recommendations across three levels of AI (LLMs, agents, agentic AI), a 5-step implementation framework, and how to stay ahead as AI reshapes the content landscape.

---

### Overview

Three parts covered:
1. Tool recommendations — what's currently being used
2. Step-by-step breakdown of how to implement AI into your content workflow
3. Where AI is going and how it affects content

---

## Part 1 — Tool Recommendations

**Three levels of AI to understand:**

**Level 1 — LLMs (Large Language Models)**
- Tools: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Perplexity
- Function: You ask a question → it gives an answer. Like a glorified Google with all the knowledge
- Recommendation: Pick 1–3 go-to tools (some are better at specific things)
- Presenter uses: Claude + Acquisition AI (trained on Alex Hormozi's content/books)

**Level 2 — AI Agents**
- Tools: Claude Cowork, Claude Code
- Function: AI executes entire workflows and tasks for you (like running an SOP). You tell it the task/workflow and it executes
- Recommendation: Claude Cowork and Claude Code

**Level 3 — Agentic AI**
- Tools: OpenClaw, Manis, and others
- Function: Doesn't follow your workflow — it plans, adapts, and solves on its own. Creates multi-step systems independently, reflects on past mistakes, and self-corrects
- Recommendation: OpenClaw (Manis is catching up). Choose 1–2 tools at this level

**Bonus Tool Recommendation — Whisper Flow**
Voice-to-text tool you install on your computer. Press a button, speak, and it perfectly formats/transcribes with grammar and corrections. Reportedly saves ~1 hour of typing per day.

---

## Part 2 — How to Implement AI into Your Content Workflow

### Step 1 — Educate the AI (LLMs)

1. **Introduce yourself:** Tell your AI who you are, your business, your role, your goals, and what you want to get out of working with it
2. **Ask for clarification:** Ask if it has any questions or needs more context
3. **Upload data sources:** Upload all past high-quality work as training data — the more quality inputs, the better the outputs

### Step 2 — Create a Master Prompt (LLMs)

*(Credit: Dan Martell)*

1. Type into your LLM: *"I want to create a master prompt for my role as [insert role]. Ask me all the questions you need to know."*
2. Answer its questions → go through the output → refine anything that doesn't feel right
3. Save as a PDF in Google Drive (name it: *master prompt — [position name]*)
4. Include this master prompt every time you ask your LLM a question so it always has full context

### Step 3 — AI Basics for LLMs

Quick tips for better LLM output:

1. **AI Hygiene** *(Credit: Hormozi)* — Start a new chat for every new topic
2. **Iterations** — The more back-and-forth within the same chat, the better the output gets (it's rarely one-and-done)
3. **Input Quality** *(Credit: Dan Martell)* — Better quality input = better quality output
4. **Prompt Formula:**
   - **Role** — Who should the AI be?
   - **Context** — Dump everything relevant in
   - **Command** — Tell it exactly what you want it to do
   - **Format** — Specify the output format (bullet list, table, PDF, etc.)

### Step 4 — Break Down Roles into Workflows (for Agents)

*(Credit: Hormozi)*

1. Choose one role to start with (e.g., your content team might have an ideator, editor, copywriter)
2. Break that role down into individual workflows/actions (a single role might have 15+ workflows; each workflow can have hundreds of micro-actions)
3. If you already have SOPs, you're ahead — they're already broken into actions
4. **Goal:** Map every role → every workflow → every action, from start to finish (linear workflow)

### Step 5 — Build an Agent for Each Workflow

*(Presenter's preferred tool: Claude)*

1. **Convert to Skill Markdown File:** Turn each SOP/workflow into its own individual skill markdown file (using a specific prompt in Claude)
2. **Test and Refine:** Run the skill on a real task → see where it breaks → correct Claude to edit the skill file → repeat until output equals or exceeds your own quality (some processes took 100+ runs to perfect)
3. **Turn into an Agent:** Once quality matches yours, convert the skill markdown into an AI agent (using a specific prompt)
4. **Map Agent Handoffs:** Write out how each agent's output becomes the input for the next agent — connecting them into a single linear workflow

Repeat for every workflow, every role, then connect roles together so they trigger each other.

---

### Advice for AI Implementation

- Do not replace tasks with AI until the AI does it as well as (or better than) you
- The more specific the skill markdown file, the better
- When correcting the AI, be ultra-specific — like talking to a toddler
  - Wrong: *"Be more authoritative"*
  - Right: *"Raise your voice like this, talk faster, puff your chest out when you speak"*

---

## Part 3 — Where AI is Going and How It Affects Content

**The big picture:**
AI will normalize the way computers did when they entered the workforce — many admin/repetitive tasks will be replaced, and jobs will look different. Learn it now and use it to your advantage.

**Content-specific notes:**

- **Ideation is still alpha:** The idea and topic of your video is still top priority — AI cannot yet automate ideation for content
- **The human element is key:** Your stories, experiences, and personal lessons are what make your scripts different — AI can't replicate this; you still need to go in and add your personal details
- **Human quality control is still needed:** Even with automated editing, someone needs to check for spelling errors, wrong words, and visual accuracy. AI can cut a 2-hour YouTube script to 20 minutes, but human QC remains essential
- **Don't outsource decision-making:** Use your own judgment for your content and business decisions

---

### How to Stand Out in the Age of "AI Slop" Content

As AI makes content easier to create, more and more videos look the same. The people winning are the ones building a personal brand:

1. Create your own content series (daily, progress update, lesson, step-by-step)
2. Develop your own viral format and consistently double down on it
3. Add social proof into your scripts (personal or client results that others can't copy)
4. Add personal stories and experiences (so others can't copy or AI can't generate it)
5. Add your personality into scripts so you don't sound like AI or everyone else
6. Be the first to post a viral video in your niche — never copy someone word for word
