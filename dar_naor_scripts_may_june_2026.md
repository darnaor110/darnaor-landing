# Dar Naor's Script Examples — Voice & Style Reference

## Key Voice Characteristics

Based on the May & June 2026 scripts:

### Tone & Delivery
- **Direct, no-bullshit:** Short sentences. Facts over fluff.
- **Educational but conversational:** Explains science simply, not condescending.
- **Contrarian when needed:** Willing to go against mainstream advice if data supports it.
- **Personal authority:** "I did this," "I experienced this," "My clients see this" — proven through lived experience.
- **Mocking of excuses:** Calls out cognitive dissonance, victim mentality, magical thinking gently but firmly.

### Structure Patterns
- **Hook:** Provocative statement or question that challenges viewer's belief
  - "What's the point of counting calories if you're gonna be miserable forever?"
  - "You don't understand because you're not fat"
  - "Processed food = dumb. Processed food = always bad" (then argue why this oversimplifies)

- **Value delivery:** Mix of:
  - Specific numbers (grams, calories, timeframes)
  - Contrasts (This vs. That, Wrong vs. Right)
  - Personal stories (my shoulder injury, my own transformation)
  - Mythbusting (debunk common beliefs with data)

- **CTA:** Always educational/philosophical, not salesy
  - "Follow for more content that assumes you're intelligent"
  - "These are the rules I teach my clients"
  - Ends with why it matters (Lean Forever philosophy)

### Language Markers
- **Specific, measurable:** "250g of rice vs. 300-400g of pasta" not "eat more protein"
- **Short declarative statements:** "That's wrong." "That's not how it works." "Here's why."
- **Hebrew-English mix in thinking, but execute pure education**
- **Avoids:** Fluff, motivation, "believe in yourself," generic fitness advice
- **Embraces:** Science, data, personal experience, behavioral psychology, systems thinking

## Script Samples (Exact Text)

### Video 1 (May) — "Calories & Protein" Theme
**Hook (Verbal):** "אם כל מה שאתם מכירים בתזונה זה קלוריות וחלבון — לא פלא שאתם לא מרוצים"
(If all you know about nutrition is calories and protein, no wonder you're not satisfied)

**Key lines:**
- "Calories, and all those other things exist" (contrast — there's more)
- "These are white lies personal trainers tell you"
- "It's good mainly because of its antioxidant content" (specific reasoning, not vague)
- "Hard to reach healthy protein amounts, so we need to know which food ONLY has protein, and which has ENOUGH to actually matter"

**Format:** Shot/angle changes, text overlays of the values popping on screen, contrasting options visually

**Editing:** Black and white, then color comes in; values appear as text

### Video 2 (June) — "Processed Food" Theme
**Hook (Verbal):** "המינוח 'מזון מעובד' הוא זלזול בוטה באינטלגנציה שלכם"
(The term 'processed food' is outright disrespect to your intelligence)

**Key lines:**
- "Even a peeled apple is 'processed'" (redefine the term)
- "The real question is: how much processing, and is it harmful?"
- "99% of being healthy is: be lean. Do strength training. Eat enough protein, veggies, fiber. Keep average steps reasonable."
- "The other 1% — ice baths, massage, supplements — won't matter if you don't nail the 99%"
- "When I was big and muscular enough, 2-3 kg fluctuation didn't shake me"

**Format:** Close-ups, visual comparisons, split screen (this vs. that), quick cuts with text

**Editing:** Graphics of foods with values jumping on screen, emphasis on specific numbers

## Messaging Pillars (How to Write for Dar)

### Pillar 1: Science-Based, Not Trend-Based
- Always cite data, experience, or observable pattern
- If debunking: show why the common belief is wrong + what's actually true
- Avoid: "wellness," "detox," "natural = better"

### Pillar 2: Actionable, Not Motivational
- End with something viewers can DO, not something to feel
- "Here's why you're not progressing" → "Here's the specific lever to pull"
- Numbers, timeframes, concrete steps

### Pillar 3: Smart Audience Assumption
- Audience is intelligent, analytical, career-driven
- Respect their time — no fluff, no 10-minute intro
- Assume they want to understand the WHY, not just follow commands

### Pillar 4: Lean Forever Philosophy
- Every video should connect back to sustainable, long-term thinking
- Not "quick fixes" or "transformation in 90 days"
- Focus on: learning to manage yourself, building systems, understanding the science

### Pillar 5: Behavioral Psychology
- Address the real blocks: mindset, cognitive dissonance, excuses, psychology
- "99% of your problem is your thinking, not the food/training"
- Call out when people are lying to themselves gently but directly

## What NOT to Do

❌ Motivational quotes or "believe in yourself" messaging
❌ Vague advice ("eat healthy," "train hard")
❌ Trends or whatever's viral (unless you're debunking it)
❌ Fake science or studies used selectively
❌ Content that assumes ignorance (your audience is smart)
❌ Long, winding stories without a point
❌ Selling dreams instead of systems

## Template for Script Writing

When writing scripts for Dar, use this structure:

1. **Hook (3 sec):** Provocative truth or question that challenges a belief
2. **Setup (few sec):** "Here's what people think... Here's the reality"
3. **Value (main body):** 
   - Specific data/numbers
   - Visual contrasts or comparisons
   - Personal example or client example
   - Why the mainstream advice is wrong
4. **Psychology/Mindset (2-3 sec):** Why this matters behaviorally
5. **CTA (final):** "Follow for more content that treats you like you're intelligent" OR case study/social proof
