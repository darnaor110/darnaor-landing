## CORE PRINCIPLE: 30-Day Profitability Rule

**Make back CAC + delivery costs within 30 days or you can't scale.**

This single rule drives every recommendation. If you don't hit 30-day profitability:
- You can't afford customer acquisition
- You'll run out of cash before customers pay you back
- You'll be forced to cut ads, get fewer customers, and eventually fail

**How this works in practice:**
- Use credit card float: spend $100 in ads Monday, customer pays you by month-end, pay credit card off → repeat infinitely
- If it takes 90 days to break even, you need 3x the cash in the bank to scale
- Without this, you're limited to organic growth (slow)

## SECTION I-PLUS: PRICING STRATEGY (The Multiplier for All Offers)

### The Power of Pricing

**Core Truth:** Raising price by 1% = 12.7% profit increase (if you keep margins steady)
- Improving retention by 1% = 6.71% profit increase
- Improving acquisition by 1% = 3.32% profit increase

**Pricing is 4x more powerful than acquisition.**

### Three Pricing Models

**1. Cost-Plus (Broken)**
- Price = Cost + arbitrary margin
- Problem: You lose everyone who'd pay more; costs change; customers don't care about your costs

**2. Competitor-Based (Lazy)**
- Price = what competitors charge (average)
- Problem: You're copying broken businesses; no strategy, no differentiation

**3. Value-Based (Right)**
- Price = what customer willingly pays for the value
- Problem: Requires you to actually deliver value (not a problem if you do)

**Move to Value-Based pricing.** It forces you to keep improving your offer to justify higher prices.

### Three Metrics to Find Your Perfect Price

If you want to know what price makes most money:

1. **Conversion rate** — how many buy at this price
2. **Churn rate** — how long they stay at this price
3. **Lifetime value** — conversion × average time × price

**Test at least 3 price points.** You're looking for the one with highest **total return** (revenue × conversion rate), not highest price or most customers.

Example: 
- $10: 5% convert, $100 LTV = $500 total
- $20: 4% convert, $200 LTV = $800 total (60% better)
- $100: 2% convert, $300 LTV = $600 total (20% better)

**Winner: $20.** Test between $20-$100 next.

### Five Rules for Raising Prices

**Rule 1: High close rate = prices too low**
If you close > 50% of sales, you have room to raise prices and still increase revenue.

**Rule 2: Full capacity = prices too low**
If you're booked solid, raise prices before adding capacity.

**Rule 3: Fixed capacity = always raise prices**
Fewer customers at higher price = more profit. If you can only serve 10 people/month and they all book, 10x your price.

**Rule 4: Pair raises with improvements**
If you raise price by 30%, your offer should improve by 30% (or message better). Otherwise it feels like a ripoff.

**Rule 5: Hear more nos, make more money**
When you raise prices, you'll hear "no" more often. This is good. It means you're closer to your actual max price.

## PRICING PLAYS: The $100M Playbook

### Play #1: Monthly to 28-Day Billing Cycles (+8.3%)

**How it works:** Bill every 4 weeks (13 cycles/year) instead of 12 months
- Monthly: 12 × $100 = $1,200/year
- 28-day: 13 × $100 = $1,300/year
- Same people buy, same conversion, 8.3% more revenue

**Implementation:** Change contracts for new customers immediately. Set a date for existing customers.

**Why it works:** People expect 12 monthly payments. 13 cycles feels completely normal and they don't mentally object.

---

### Play #2: Processing Fees + Second Payment Method (+3-4%)

**How it works:** After they agree to price, say "Just a 3.99% credit card processing fee" OR "Save the fee by giving us a second form of payment (ACH, bank transfer)"

**Why:** Involuntary churn = 1.2-1.7% monthly (cards decline, need replacement). Offering 2nd form saves you that.

**Example math (with $100/month, 5% churn):**
- Add fee: $104/mo, 5% churn = $2,080 LTV (+4%)
- Add 2nd card: $100/mo, 3.8% churn (save 1.2%) = $2,631 LTV (+31%)

**People rarely object.** Most see it as reasonable. Just mention it after they commit to the price.

### Play #3: Sales Tax Buildup (0-10%)

**How it works:** Add line item for sales tax at checkout (if your state/service requires it)

**Psychology:** Present as separate line, matter-of-fact tone
> "State tax code [1030a.o] mandates 6% sales tax."

**Key rule:** You can ONLY charge taxes you actually pay. Check with a lawyer.

**Why it works:** People hate blaming taxes. They blame the government, not you. This is one of the easiest adds.

### Play #4: Annual Price Increases (3-10%)

**How it works:** Build annual increases into new customer contracts (3-15% per year)

**Math example:**
- Year 0: $100
- Year 1: $105 (5% raise)
- Year 2: $110 (cumulative 10%)
- Year 3: $116 (cumulative 16%)
- Year 4: $122 (cumulative 22%)

**Why:** Inflation eats margins. If you don't raise prices, you lose 20%+ purchasing power every 7 years.

**Implementation:** Add to contracts as "rate protection clause." New customers expect this. Existing customers, you implement on renewal.

**Reference:** Warren Buffett raised See's Candies prices 50+ times over 50 years, turning it into a $1B+ profit business.

### Play #5: Longer Duration Billing (+10-15%)

**How it works:** Offer annual instead of monthly. People stay longer when they pay upfront.

**Churn impact (from Profitwell data):**
- 1x per year: 2% monthly churn
- 4x per year (quarterly): 5% monthly churn
- 12x per year (monthly): 10.7% monthly churn

**LTV math:**
- Monthly at $100: 10.7% churn = $935 LTV
- Quarterly at $100: 5% churn = $2,000 LTV (2.14x better)
- Annual at $100: 2% churn = $5,000 LTV (5.35x better)

**Offer structure:**
1. First ask: "Full price for the year?" ($1,200)
2. If hesitate: "Want a 16% discount for annual?" ($1,000)
3. If still no: "What about quarterly?" ($300)
4. If still no: Monthly with no discount

**Pro tip:** Always start HIGH with annual price, then discount down. Never start low.

### Play #6: Round Up Pricing (+1-3%)

**How it works:** Change all prices to end in .99 instead of .00, or change 7s to 9s

Examples:
- $47/week → $49.99/week
- $37/week → $39.99/week
- $27/week → $29.99/week

**Result:** 6-11% revenue increase with ZERO impact on conversion
(Luxury items: keep round numbers — $5,000 not $4,999)

**Implementation:** Update contracts for new customers immediately.

- Added LTV: $1,800 per customer

### Play #9: Ultra-High Anchor Offer (+10-15%)

**How it works:** Offer 10x expensive version first, watch them gasp, rescue with main offer

**Psychology:** Price anchoring works. First number heard becomes reference point.

**Flow:**

2. (They gasp — this is working)
3. "Do you care about designer branding?"
4. When they say no: "Great, we have a $2,200 option that does the same core stuff"
5. Feels like a steal

**Key:** The expensive one MUST be real (some will buy it). You're not faking it.

**Benefit:** People who would've said yes to $5,000 now spend $2,200 and feel grateful. Meanwhile, some people buy the $16,000 option.

### Play #10: Guarantee/Warranty Upsell (+5-20%)

**How it works:** After they buy, offer extended guarantee for 5-30% of price

**Example:** $1,000 product → $100 guarantee "no watermarks or we replace"

**Math:** If 1 in 20 people claim, and cost to fix is $100:
- Revenue from guarantees: 20 × $100 = $2,000
- Cost of one claim: $100
- Net: $1,900 pure profit

**Why it works:** People hate risk. They'll pay to transfer risk to you. And most never claim it.

**Psychology:** The smaller the guarantee price, the more people take it. Even 2% warranty adds up.

## SECTION I-PLUS-PLUS: PRICE RAISE PLAYBOOK (How to Raise Without Losing Customers)

### The Real Problem with Not Raising Prices

Most business owners fear raising prices so they don't. Result:
- Margins erode due to inflation
- Business gets worse (can't afford to improve product)
- They eventually go broke

### The Raise Sequence: RAISE

Use this format when raising prices on existing customers.

**R — Remind** (them of value received)
"Over the past 12 months, we've:
- Made you X
- Feature A helped you do Y
- You've used Feature B daily"

**A — Address** (the price change directly)
"For us to continue investing in making this for you, we need to increase our prices."

**I — Invest** (tell them where the money goes)
"With extra resources, we're going to:
- Expand Thing A (you love it)
- Hire people to improve B
- New equipment/tech/training"

**S — Soften** (with a loyalty discount)
"You've been loyal for X months. So we're keeping you on your old price for the next 3-6 months. We're giving you [$XXX] credit as a thank you."

**E — Explain** (away their concerns)
"If you have questions, reply to me directly. PS: If this materially affects your business/groceries, let me know and we'll work something out."

### How to Pick Your New Price

**Test with new customers first.**

Run the new price on fresh acquisitions for 2 weeks. If they buy at reasonable rates and stay (low churn), roll it to existing customers.

**Do the math:**

If you raise price 20% and lose 15% of customers:
- Old: 100 customers × $100 = $10,000/month
- New: 85 customers × $120 = $10,200/month (2% better)

**You make more money.** This is normal. You'll hear "no" more, but you'll make more.

### Rules for Raising Without Disaster

**Rule 1: Don't grandfather existing customers (don't lock prices)**
You never know what you'll offer in the future. Lifetime deals = worst business decision.

**Rule 2: Never sell "lifetime access for one-time payment"**
Unless it costs zero to fulfill. You'll end up broke. Every large company has tried this and failed.

**Rule 3: Raise at least once per year**
Most businesses should raise 5-15% annually. At minimum 3% for inflation.

**Rule 4: Test before rolling out**
New customers first → gather data → prove it works → then tell existing customers.

**Rule 5: Pair raises with product improvements**
30% price raise = offer something 30% better (or better messaging of what already exists).

**Rule 6: High close rate = room to raise**
If you're closing > 50% of sales, you're priced too low. Keep raising until you hit 30-40% close rate.

### The Psychology of Raising Prices

**Virtuous cycle (when you raise prices):**
- Customers have higher emotional investment
- They perceive more value
- Results improve (placebo + better customer quality)
- Less demanding customers (broke people ask for more)
- You can afford to serve them better

**Vicious cycle (when you keep prices low):**
- Low emotional investment
- Low perceived value
- Worse results
- Most demanding customers (cheap people complain most)
- Can't afford to improve, spiral continues

**Choose the virtuous cycle.** Raise prices.

### The Three Types of Responses

When you announce a price raise:

**Type 1: Get the value (70-80%)**
They see the value, accept it. May grumble but comply.

**Type 2: Actually affected (5-10%)**
Sob stories. Legitimately hurt by the raise. Solution: extend the discount another 6 months.

**Type 3: Already leaving anyway (5-10%)**
Would've canceled next month anyway. You "pulled forward" their churn by a month. You lose one month of revenue, not three.

**Result:** Only lose 5-15% of customers to a price raise. Meanwhile revenue goes up 20%+.

### Win Your Money Back

**Structure:** Customer pays upfront. Gets money back (cash or credit) if they hit a goal OR take required actions OR both.

**Best for:** Programs where customer effort = results (fitness, education, business coaching)

**Three options:**

1. **Results-based:** Get X result (lose 20 lbs, make $5k/month, acquire 5 customers) → money back
2. **Action-based:** Attend all calls, log progress, do homework → money back  
3. **Both:** Hit result AND follow actions → money back

**Refund rate target:** ~10% or lower. If higher, your product is broken—fix it first before running this.

**Key mechanics:**
- Make winning criteria **easy to track** (use existing systems: phones for steps, Slack for messages, cameras for photos)
- Align criteria with customer goals (attendance, homework, posting = what makes great customers)
- Make advertising part of the criteria (post results, tag you, refer friends)
- **Apply store credit over time, not up front:** $600 won back → $50/month discount for 12 months. Keeps them engaged longer than 3 free months

**Upsell into the credit:**
- During progress checks (before/middle/after), offer what they need next
- Frame credit application to longer-term offers

**Late-stage rescue:** If someone fails but has clearly tried, tell them:
> "You won. You started. That's the biggest victory. Credit your entire deposit toward staying long-term."

Turn loss into goodwill.

**Data from thousands of gyms:**
- $10,000+ businesses use this
- Generates massive advertising (winners promote you for free)
- Real money comes from upsells to people who succeed, not from refund savings

### Giveaways

**Structure:** Grand Prize (your main offer) + Sweepstakes → Non-winners get promotional discount

**Flow:**
1. Advertise grand prize with anchored value (e.g., "$5,000 value")
2. Collect entries + eligibility questions
3. Pick winner publicly
4. Contact non-winners privately: "You qualified for something..."
5. Offer promotional version at discount

**Entry criteria = lead qualification intel:**
- "Why should we pick you?"
- "What's your goal?"
- "Why does this matter to you?"

**Deadline mechanics (create urgency):**
- 3-7 days to enter
- 7 days to claim promotional offer after announcement
- Daily emails showing benefits + countdown

**Pricing math:**
- Grand Prize value: $5,000
- Retail price: $2,000
- Discount to non-winners: 10-30% off retail

Example: "You qualified for $5,000 in value for $1,800" (highlighting the $5k, not just the discount)

**Key tactics:**
- Give away 2 grand prizes (if someone they referred wins, they win too) = double leads + invested referrers
- Scarcity on entries: Cap by time (7 days) OR number (5,000 entries)—limit to people you can call in 7 days
- Have downsells ready: If they don't take main offer, offer discount on different product

**Legal note:** Consult counsel. Make sure actual winner receives grand prize. Rules must be clear.

### Decoy Offer

**Structure:** Advertise free/cheap basic version (decoy) → Present premium version alongside it

**Psychology:** Contrast makes premium look like obvious choice

**Flow:**
1. Advertise decoy (free 6-week program, $21 fitness challenge, free access to basic materials)
2. When they engage, present both options
3. **Ask permission first:** "Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?"
   - If "results" → skip decoy, present premium directly
   - If "free stuff" → present decoy, then immediately contrast with premium
4. Ask "Which would get you better results?"

**Decoy design:**
- Strip down hard (older tech, fewer features, no guarantees, generic vs. personalized)
- Make premium shine by comparison
- But don't make decoy *useless* (keep it viable)

**Decoy examples:**
- **Gym:** Free 1-session/week in group class OR $399/month unlimited + 1-1 coaching + guarantee
- **Course:** Free basic access in Discord OR $299 with personalized feedback + certification
- **Service:** DIY version (we send you tools) OR Done-For-You (we do it + results guaranteed)

**Conversion note:** Discount offers get fewer leads but higher show-up rates. Free offers get more leads but lower show-up. Test for your business.

**Surprise benefit tactic:** If they take decoy, throw in a low-cost premium feature anyway → builds goodwill, increases chance of upsell later.

### Buy X Get Y Free

**Structure:** Customer buys 1, gets 2 free (or any ratio). Reframe discount as free stuff.

**Psychology:** "Free" drives more interest than discount percentage

Example: "Buy 1 shirt for $20, Get 2 Free" vs. "Buy 3 shirts for $20 (33% off)"
- Same math, but free moves more units

**Pricing setup:**
- Raise base price BEFORE using this offer (so margin survives)
- Boot Factory: normally $200/pair → marked up to $600 → "Buy 1 Get 2 Free" = 3 pairs for $600 = still $200/pair

**Key principle:** Give more free than paid when possible
- "Buy 1 Get 2 Free" > "Buy 2 Get 1 Free"

**Multiple offers:** Get creative with what's free
- Can be same product or different products
- "Buy Shirt Get Socks Free"
- "Buy 1 Month Service Get 3 Months Free"

**For recurring:** 
- "Buy 9 months Get 9 Months Free" = 18 months total = same cash flow, way better offer
- Extends retention (customers stay longer)

**Critical:** You must deliver. Budget cash to service everyone for the full duration. Don't be a fraud.

**For existing customers:** Sell prepaid blocks to current recurring customers (limit to 10% of base so you don't destroy cash flow)

**For one-time buyers:** If customer only buys once, maximize that purchase. They'll never come back, so make it BIG.

### Pay Less Now or Pay More Later

**Structure:** Choice A (pay full price later) OR Choice B (pay less now + get bonuses)

**Flow:**
1. Get card on file (no risk to customer)
2. Offer "Pay $0 now, get billed $X in 14 days" (free trial)
3. Add condition: Must [attend / show up / do X] to qualify for guarantee
4. Follow up with "Pay Now" option: "$97 today + bonuses" instead of full price later

**Key mechanics:**
- Promise a **clear, measurable yes/no result** (pain scale 1-10, before/after photo, income target)
- Make attendance/participation requirement = what makes great customers
- If >10% cancel billing, you promised too much or the guarantee conditions were too loose

**Bonuses for "pay now":** 
- Recordings they can't buy elsewhere
- Access to expert consultation
- Exclusive materials
- Delivery acceleration (get it sooner)

**Bonus pricing:** Make "pay now" price 20-50% discount from full price

**For recurring:** Offer same logic but on subscription level
- "Pay $99/month recurring OR $297 upfront today + lifetime lower rate"

**Hint at next offer early:**
> "Everyone wants to know when the next intensive starts because they sell out. I'll get to it after... please pay attention to this first."

This creates anticipation without selling it yet—increases uptake when you do present it.

## SECTION III: UPSELL OFFERS (Offer the next thing)

### The Classic Upsell

**Core:** Identify problem your offer reveals → solve it immediately

**Formula:** "You can't have X without Y"

**Examples:**
- Car rental reveals: need insurance, sealant, late return, prepaid gas
- Gym reveals: need supplements, nutrition coaching, better class times  
- Bike sale reveals: need helmet, lights, lock, tubes

**Timing:** Right when the problem appears (they've already bought, emotions high, wallet out)

**Execution tactics:**

1. **Offer more profitable upsells first** (if 2 options, lead with higher margin)

2. **Get them to say no to say yes:**
   > "You don't want anything else besides this, do you?"
   
   People are conditioned to say "no." Turn that into consent.

3. **Give access before they say yes** (hard to give something back)

4. **Surprise & delight:** Have 4 bonuses saved. Add 1 at a time if they hesitate. If they say yes before all 4, give all 4 anyway.

5. **Hyper buying cycle:** People spend most in first 30 days of new commitment (new hobby, wedding, baby, move)
   - Don't shy away from multiple upsells during this window
   - Keep making offers

6. **Use free bonuses to create upsell problems:**
   - Give free earmuffs → they need storage for them → upsell storage fee
   - Free onboarding → they need ongoing support → upsell support plan

7. **BAMFAM (Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting):** End every appointment with next appointment scheduled
   - They should know why they're coming back before leaving
   > "Your next appointment is Tuesday at 10am to review your nutrition plan. We'll also talk about supplements. Sound good?"

8. **Bundle upsells & name them:** Instead of offering 9 things, offer "Transformation Package" (3 things) or "VIP Bundle" (5 things)
   - Makes it easier to sell

9. **Integrate into delivery:** When teaching nutrition, mention supplements. When teaching marketing, mention software tools.
   - More people ask for them naturally

10. **Bundle pricing to push medium tier:**
    - Small $5, Medium $8 (should be $7), Large $9 → most pick Large
    - Small $6, Medium $7, Large $9 → most pick Medium
    
    (Pricing the middle option at slightly lower than you'd expect shifts volume)

### Menu Upsell

**Structure:** Unsell → Prescribe → A/B Choice → Card on File

**Step 1: Unsell (tell them what they DON'T need)**
> "You don't need the basic plan. You don't need these vitamins—the food already has them."

Builds goodwill. Makes premium solutions obvious.

**Step 2: Prescribe (tell them exactly what to do)**
> "You're going to heat up first, then trigger point work, then dynamic stretches. Here—I wrote it down so you don't forget."

Remove the option of not buying. Act like they already have it.

**Step 3: A/B Choice (pick between two yeses)**
Instead of: "Do you want X?" → "Do you want X or Y?"

Examples:
- "Chocolate or vanilla?" (flavor choice)
- "Before work or after?" (timing choice)  
- "Email or phone?" (communication choice)
- "Standard or overnight?" (speed choice)

**Nudge toward one:** "This one is usually the safe bet" or "My personal favorite is..."

**Step 4: Card on File**
> "Wanna just use the card we have on file?"

Don't ask for payment. Assume payment. Lower the friction.

**The "Economist Play" (menu psychology):**

If you have 2 options and want people to buy both, add a decoy:
- Digital only: $59/year
- Print only: $125/year  
- Digital + Print: $125/year ← Most people pick this (same price as Print, but more value)

**For first purchase:** Ask "What card would you like to use?" instead of "Do you want to pay?"

### Anchor Upsell

**Structure:** Expensive option → Gasp → Rescue → Main offer → Payment

**Step 1: Present Anchor (5-10x price of main offer)**
> "$16,000 suit with Italian silk, hand-stitched..."

Expect panic.

**Step 2: Get "The Gasp"** (mini panic attack = anchor working)

**Step 3: Come to Rescue**
> "Do you care much about being a designer brand?"

When they say no, you already have backup option ready.

**Step 4: Present Main Offer** (5-10x cheaper)
> "$2,200 suit. Same primary features, different secondary ones."

Feels like steal.

**Step 5: Payment**
> "What card would you like?"

**Key mechanics:**
- Anchor and main must have same **primary** features (both suits fit, both protect you)
- Remove **secondary** features to justify price cut (designer, material prestige, personalization)
- Actually sell the anchor (don't phone it in) → some will buy it
- Anchored customers spend more than they planned, even on the "cheap" option
- Biggest advantage: some customers buy the premium → outsized profit from few sales

**Pro tip:** Don't lose customers by offering premium first to someone with $100 budget. But don't offer $100 option to someone with $1,000 budget either.

### Rollover Upsell

**Structure:** Credit previous purchase toward next (more expensive) offer

**Four situations:**

1. **Re-engage old customers:** 
   > "I saw you haven't been with us in 6 months. I want to give you $500 of credit back toward staying long-term. Fair enough?"

2. **Rescue upset customers:**
   Instead of refund, offer credit toward different product

3. **Steal competitors' upset customers:**
   > "I saw the bad review. Here's what—I'll credit whatever you have left with them toward switching to us. You don't lose anything, start getting value now."

4. **Upsell current customers:**
   They bought 1 month → offer credit toward 6-month commitment

**How to apply credit:**
- **Upfront:** Full credit applied now (they pay less immediately)
- **Spread:** $600 credit ÷ 12 months = $50/month discount for a year

Spreading keeps skin in the game. People stick better.

**Pricing rule:**
- Make next offer at least **4x the credit** (minimum 25% discount)
- Example: $600 credit toward $2,400 offer = $1,800 out of pocket

**Urgency:** Make it one-time only
> "This offer expires today. Take it now or full price later."

**"Winback campaign" example:**
- Make personalized video to 200 past customers offering $4,000 credit
- 20% accepted = ~$1.9M additional annual revenue
- Two days of video = massive return

**Gift card play (generate new leads):**
- Sell $200 gift card for $20 (90% off)
- Limit 2 per customer
- Rule: only redeemable by recipient on other people
- When they redeem, roll the $200 toward a $1,000+ offer
- You get: referrals + full-price upsell

## SECTION IV: DOWNSELL OFFERS (When they say no)

### Payment Plan Downsells

**Core:** Same offer, different payment timing

**The 7-Step Seesaw:**

1. **Reward for paying full:** "Normally $5,000. Pay today, get it for $3,500."
   - Not interest-based (don't punish payment plans)
   - Discount-based (reward early payment)

2. **Offer 3rd-party financing:** Let external company pay you, customer pays them over time
   - You get cash today
   - Customer gets easy payment plan

3. **Credit card option:** "Want to decide your own payment terms? Use a credit card and pay them over time."
   - Reframe: you're not making them pay in parts, they're choosing to

4. **Half now, half later:** "When do you get paid next? Let's do half today, half then."
   - Ask about paycheck dates (2 weeks > monthly for completion)
   - Much higher success than arbitrary monthly

5. **Temperature check:** "On a scale 1-10, how bad do you want this?"
   - 8+: Keep downselling
   - 7-: "What would a 10 look like?" → offer different product

6. **Three payments:** "⅓ now, ⅓ next paycheck, ⅓ the one after"

7. **Spread across service:** 16-week program = charge weekly (eliminates "monthly" psychology)

**If still no:** Free Trial with Penalty (next chapter)

**Critical mechanics:**

- **Align with paychecks, not calendar:** Biweekly > Monthly (much higher completion)
- **Bill every 4 weeks, not 30 days:** 13 billing cycles/year instead of 12 = 8.3% instant profit boost
  - Same number of people buy
  - No more work
  - Literally changed Alex's life (millions in extra profit)

- **Run declined payments multiple times same day:** Deposits clear at different times. Recoup ~⅓ of declined payments this way

- **Built-in upsell:** During payment plan, offer original "prepay discount" if they pay balance early
  - Incentivizes faster payment

- **Make sure more people close:** If close rate goes from 3 of 10 to 6 of 10 (with 3 still paying full), you doubled customers AND got cash from all 6. That's the win.

**Get two forms of payment:**
> "Wanna save the processing fee? Give us a second form of payment in case the first one declines."

People rarely object. Gets you ACH (cheaper to process).

**The seesaw phrasing (simplified):**
> "Here's the deal. Normally this costs more. BUT if you prepay it today, huge discount. OR we can do how much per month can you do?"

Let them pick payment size, you pick duration.

### Trial With Penalty

**Structure:** Free if you do X, Y, Z. Fee if you don't.

**When to use:** Downsell (they said no to main offer)

**How it works:**
1. Get credit card upfront
2. They try free (as long as they meet terms)
3. If they hit terms → charges waived, they've done what makes great customers
4. If they don't → they pay fee

**Sample terms:**
- Attend all calls
- Log daily progress
- Do homework
- Post in group weekly
- Come to check-in meetings

**Key insight:** Same criteria as Win Your Money Back offer = what makes great customers anyway

**Fee structure:**
- Can be 1 big fee if they miss (simplest)
- Or break it up: $50 per missed call, $25 per missed homework
- Smaller fees = easier psychology, but more admin

**5-step downsell process:**

1. **Ask permission:** "How about we get you started for free? You'd just need to [do X, Y, Z]"

2. **Get card:** Don't ask "Do you agree to fees?" → Ask "What card do you wanna use?"
   - Get card first. Explain fees after.

3. **Sell staying & paying:**
   > "If we get you results, will you stay long-term?"
   
   If no → don't do trial. Not worth hassle.

4. **Explain fees as motivation:**
   > "We charge to keep you accountable. You're betting on yourself. Miss stuff, we charge to get you back on track."

5. **Make check-ins required:** Meetings become upsell opportunities
   - Check-in 1: Offer supplement/nutrition upsell
   - Check-in 2: Offer membership/continuity upsell

**Three outcomes when trial ends:**

1. **They succeeded:** Easy upsell to longer/higher version
2. **They hated it:** Ask what would be different, offer something better suited (often upsell)
3. **They didn't use it:** Reach out multiple times, offer to waive fee if they do exit interview, or offer what they actually need

**Just call it "Free Trial":**
Penalties confuse people. Plain English works.

**Works for:** Recurring programs where customer effort = results
- Don't use for one-time things (courses should use Pay Less Now/Pay More Later)

**Pro tip:** First month might be free, then $X/month. Or $1 first month, then full price. Gets card on file without scaring them.

### Feature Downsells

**Structure:** Lower price by removing features (not by discounting same thing)

**Psychology:** People see value in what you removed once they see the price difference

**Example from book:**
> "Money-back guarantee included normally? If you don't want it, I can knock $400 off. What makes sense for you?"

Customer: "Actually, I'd rather keep the guarantee." → Sold full price

**The removal order (highest to lowest value):**
1. Quantity: 4 sessions/month → 2 sessions/month
2. Quality: Premium feature → basic feature
3. Speed: 5-minute response → overnight response  
4. Availability: 24/7 → business hours only
5. Service level: 1-1 → small group
6. Guarantees: Money-back → no refund
7. Time: Access for 1 year → access for 90 days
8. Personalization: Custom → generic
9. Done-For-You → Done-With-You → DIY

**Key principle:**
Remove features from highest value down. This incentivizes them to take the full offer instead of downsell.

If you remove low-value features first, they'll happily take the downsell and you lose money.

**Downsell progression:**

1. **First downsell:** Remove something valuable, drop price moderately
   > "Interested? Here's another option that still works—just without [premium feature]. Saves you $X. Fair enough?"

2. **If still no:** Feature Downsell + Payment Plan combo
   - Remove feature AND spread payments
   - Extremely effective

3. **Temperature check after 2 downsells:** 
   > "On a 1-10, how bad do you want this?"
   
   8+: Keep downselling
   7-: "What would make it a 10?" → offer different product entirely

**Naming packages (psychological anchoring):**
- Premium/Whale Package (aspirational name, highest tier)
- Standard/Professional (mid-tier)
- Minimum/Basic (lowest)

Minimum implies "you gotta get at least this"

**DIY Option (final downsell):**
> "I can't afford Done-For-You. Can I do it myself?"

Sell them tools/templates/training to DIY

**Current customer downsell:**
Before they cancel, offer lower tier
> "Not using the premium features? Here's a package for just what you use."

Second-highest lifetime value customers come from downselled current customers (when there's alignment).

**Barter instead of discount:**
> "I'll knock $100 off if you: 1) Leave reviews, 2) Make a testimonial video, 3) Post about progress weekly, 4) Refer 2 friends."

To you: testimonials + referrals > $100 discount
To customer: $100 < value of not advertising

## SECTION V: CONTINUITY OFFERS (Keep them buying)

### Continuity Bonus Offers

**Structure:** Join membership today → get awesome bonus for free

**Bonus value:** Usually > first month payment

**Examples:**
- $200/month membership + $1,000 onboarding free
- $59/month dog food + $800 in toys/accessories free
- $399/month newsletter + all past 40 issues free ($15,880 value)

**Advertising principle:**
Advertise the bonus, not the membership.
> "Get $1,000 of exclusive training free by becoming a member today"

Not: "Join our membership"

**Key mechanics:**

1. **Anchor bonus value first:**
   > "This training normally costs $1,000. But if you join today, you get it as a free gift."
   
   Show the value BEFORE explaining membership

2. **Then offer membership as the way to unlock it:**
   > "Here's how you can get it free: become a VIP member for $200/month."

3. **Stack bonuses:**
   > "On top of that, members get better class times ($400/month value), VIP events ($500/month value), and early access to new releases ($300/month value). That's $1,200+ free value just for joining today."

4. **Make bonus solo offer too:**
   Bonus-only option: Pay $500 (one-time)
   OR Bonus + Membership: $200/month

   Standalone needs to be 1.33x-2.66x more expensive depending on mix you want.

**Pricing formula (to split buyer preferences):**

| Target % on Continuity | Standalone Price (vs monthly) |
|---|---|
| 50% | 1.33x monthly |
| 60% | 1.66x monthly |
| 70% | 2x monthly |
| 80% | 2.33x monthly |
| 90% | 2.66x monthly |

Example: $199/month membership
- For 50/50 split: Standalone = $265
- For 70/30 split: Standalone = $398
- For 90/10 split: Standalone = $530

**Bonus sourcing:**
- Things you already have/do (past newsletters, onboarding you do anyway)
- Complementary products (opposite gender/type of main offer)
- Time/access (recordings, exclusive community)

**Don't make bonuses unrelated stuff:**
Free t-shirt for gym doesn't work. Free gym workout plan with fitness app does.

**Lifetime discount bonus:**
After 3-6-12 months, they get lifetime lower rate if they stay
> "You've earned a lifetime discount. From now on, it's $199/month instead of $249."

**Titles as bonuses:**
Some people care more about "Gold Member" status than any physical bonus

**Bulk prepay upsell (critical):**
> "Buy 6 months, get 6 months free" OR "6 months for the price of 3"

If only 1 in 8 people take this, you raise 30-day profits by 50%.

Can make or break your Money Model.

### Continuity Discount Offers

**Structure:** Commit to X duration → get Y time free

**Four ways to apply discount:**

1. **Up front:** Free time at start, then billing begins
   - Higher churn risk
   - More conversion

2. **At the end:** Earn free time by staying committed
   - Lower churn risk
   - Less conversion

3. **Spread over time:** $600 discount ÷ 12 months = $50/month off (longer-term discount applied monthly)

4. **After first 1-2 payments:** Pay first month, then get free month as bonus

**Key numbers:**
- Monthly churn: 10.7%
- Quarterly churn: 5%
- Annual churn: 2%

Longer commitments = exponentially lower churn

**THE HIGHEST-VALUE TIP IN THIS BOOK:**
Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly = 13 cycles/year instead of 12 = 8.3% instant revenue boost

Same number of people buy. No extra work. Just change calendar cycle.

Mathematically: If 20% margins, this adds 41% to annual profit.

**Gift card play:**
Give discount as physical gift card
- Customer applies whenever they want (after month 3+)
- Can gift to friend → new lead + full-price signup
- Many forget to use → you keep full price

**Lifetime discount at churn point:**
Identify your average churn month (e.g., month 4)
Advertise: "Get 30% off forever after month 4"
→ People push through the hard period to unlock it

**Cancellation mechanics:**

- **Make cancellation easy:** Hard cancellation = bad reviews
- **Exit interview incentive:** "Waive the cancellation fee if you tell me what went wrong"
  - Often save them by solving the real problem
  - At minimum, get feedback to improve

- **Cancellation fee = waived discount:**
  If they got $600 in discounts, $600 fee to cancel
  - Simple to explain
  - Fair (gets them back to full price if they leave)

- **Make sure people know how to cancel:**
  No way to cancel = they disappear and complain online

### Waived Fee Offers

**Structure:** Month-to-month with $X setup fee, OR waive fee if you commit to 12+ months

**Psychology:** People will stay longer if leaving costs more than staying

**Example:**
- Month-to-month: $1,000/month + $5,000 setup fee (can cancel anytime)
- 12-month commitment: $1,000/month, waive $5,000 fee (pay fee if you break early)

**Key mechanics:**
- **Fee size:** 3-5x monthly rate
- **Commitment length:** Minimum 1 year (works better with 2+ years)
- **Fee gets them to start:** Avoid fee → commit
- **Fee gets them to stick:** Sunk cost + leaving costs more than staying

**Presenting:**
> "You have two ways in: month-to-month with a $5,000 setup, or lock in a year and I waive it. Most people choose the year to avoid the fee."

**Math of why it works:**
- Month 1-2: Cost to leave ($5,000) > motivation to leave → stay
- Month 6: Same cost to leave, but they've gotten attached → stay
- By month 12: Cost to leave exceeds any remaining motivation → they complete commitment

Then after 12 months, waive the fee permanently.

**If >5% want to cancel early:** Product problem, not pricing problem. Fix the product.

**For more upfront cash:** Make fee smaller (1.5-3x monthly)
- More people choose month-to-month
- You get more cash now
- But less commitment

**Donate the fee to cause customer hates:**
> "If you cancel early, I'm donating your $5,000 fee to [political cause you oppose]"

Gives two reasons to stay (avoid paying, avoid supporting cause they hate)

## MONEY MODEL ASSEMBLY

### The Sequence (Stage by Stage)

**Stage I: Get Cash (Attraction Offers)**
Goal: Turn strangers → customers, cover CAC

Do ONE offer. Get good. Make it automatic. Then add next stage.

**Stage II: Get More Cash (Upsells + Downsells)**
Goal: 30-day profit > CAC + delivery + margin buffer

Upsell: what you offer next (more, better, different)
Downsell: when they say no (payment plan, trial, feature reduction)

Alternate between them in same conversation.

**Stage III: Get The Most Cash (Continuity)**
Goal: Recurring revenue + maximize customer lifetime value

Don't try this too early. Only after Stage I & II reliable.

After customer says yes to Attraction/Upsell/Downsell, THEN offer continuity.
Then upsell bulk prepay of continuity.

### Example: Gym Launch's $100M Money Model

**Stage I:**
- Free courses + calls → Decoy Offer
- DIY path (free) vs. "We implement" ($16,000/16 weeks)

**Stage II Upsell:**
- "You know the tactics, now you need the advanced playbook"
- Gym Lords: $42,000/year ($36,000 prepaid discount)
- Community access to share best practices (Continuity Bonus)

**Stage II Downsell:**
- Payment Plan Seesaw: $10k down → 8 payments → $800/week x 52

**Stage II Menu + Feature:**
- Done-For-You Ads + Sales Training + Minimum Access: Pick 1+ features at different price points

**Stage III Continuity:**
- After they buy Gym Lords or Minimum, roll into month-to-month

Result: $476k/mo (Decoy) → $1.5M/mo (Upsells) → $2.3M/mo (Menu+Feature) → $4.4M/mo (added another business)

### Building Your Model

1. **Perfect Attraction Offer first**
   - Run it for months until reliable
   - Raise price slowly
   - Optimize messaging

2. **Add Upsell**
   - Identify problem your offer creates
   - Solve it during their "hyper buying cycle"
   - Make more money per customer

3. **Add Downsells**
   - For everyone who says no
   - Start with payment plans (keep same offer)
   - Move to feature downsells (different offer)

4. **Add Continuity (last)**
   - Only after earlier stages consistent
   - Roll upsell buyers into recurring
   - Then upsell bulk prepay

**Critical:** Don't build 100 products. Build 100 ways to offer the same product.

Same fitness offering: 1x/week vs. 2x/week vs. 4x/week vs. nutrition add-on vs. supplement bundle vs. 3-month vs. 6-month vs. 12-month vs. payment plan

**Pricing progression:**
- Launch low (more yeses = feedback)
- Improve based on feedback
- Raise price slowly
- Stop when revenue drops (found your ceiling)

**Affiliate relationships fill gaps:**
Don't have supplement? Offer someone else's. Get commission. No delivery work.

## CRITICAL MECHANICS (Across All Offers)

### Card On File
Getting a card = 10x more commitment
- Drastically improves follow-through
- People who commit financially stay longer
- Always ask "What card would you like?" not "Do you want to pay?"

### Payment Timing
- Weekly > biweekly > monthly
- More frequent = more stick
- Align with paycheck dates (Thu/Fri vs. random day)

### Free Trial vs. Paid Trial
- Free trial: Lower conversion initially
- $1 trial: Gets card, higher initial conversion
- Free with card on file: Sweet spot

### Why People Say No
Not "I don't want it" but usually:
1. "I can't afford it" (usually means "can't afford it NOW")
2. "I don't understand how it works"
3. "I don't believe it'll work"
4. "It's not right for me"

Downsells address #1. Demos address #2. Guarantees address #3. Menu upsells address #4.

### The Temperature Check
After 2 downsells, ask 1-10
- 8+: Keep going, they want it
- 7-: Stop. Offer different product.

Prevents wasting time on unmotivated prospects.

### Seesaw (Start High, Work Down)
- Present expensive option first
- Come down in price/terms until they buy
- NEVER start cheap and raise price

Starting cheap trains them to negotiate down. Starting expensive teaches them to compromise upward.

## WHAT KILLS MONEY MODELS

1. **No Upsell** (leaving 70-80% revenue on table)
2. **No Downsells** (losing everyone who says no)
3. **No Continuity** (missing recurring revenue)
4. **Attraction Offer unprofitable by day 30** (can't scale, cash dies)

## WHAT SAVES MONEY MODELS

1. **Solve real problems, not symptoms they complain about**
2. **Get paid faster than you deliver**
3. **Multiple offers in sequence, not one big offer**
4. **Test simple first, fancy later**
5. **Make 30-day profitability non-negotiable**
