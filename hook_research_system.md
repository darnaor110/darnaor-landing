# Hook Research System — Dar Naor

## Purpose
When Dar sends a sketch, I research Ava's hook bank and present **3 complete hook stacks** (verbal + written + visual for each option).

---

## The 3 Hook Elements (Per Ava)

### 1. VERBAL HOOK (What viewers hear — first 3 seconds)
- The opening line you speak
- Should stop the scroll, make them curious
- Examples: "Everyone says X, but...", "If you believe this, you're wrong"

### 2. WRITTEN HOOK (What appears on screen — first 3 seconds)
- Text overlay or on-screen text
- Short, punchy, creates curiosity gap
- Examples: "3 Food Lies", "The Truth About Processed Food", "You're Doing This Wrong"

### 3. VISUAL HOOK (What viewers see — first 3 seconds)
- Camera angle, B-roll, graphics, movement
- Should match the verbal/written hooks
- Examples: "Split screen comparing two foods", "Close-up of processed vs. real food", "Text popping on screen with values"

---

For each sketch, I'll provide **3 complete stacks** organized like this:

```
├─ Written Hook: "..."
└─ Visual Hook: "..."

OPTION 3
├─ Verbal Hook: "..."
├─ Written Hook: "..."
└─ Visual Hook: "..."
```

Each option should feel like a complete, cohesive thought — not three unrelated elements.

## How I'll Search (Updated Process)

When you give me a sketch with:
- **Script Angle:** (e.g., Comparison)
- **Content Type:** (e.g., Educational)
- **Funnel Type:** (e.g., TOFU)
- **Avatar Pain Point:** (e.g., "don't know which restaurants help or hurt")
- **Topic Domain:** (e.g., fitness/nutrition/restaurants/food)

I will:

1. **Identify the topic domain** — What niche is this video about? (fitness, nutrition, food, restaurants, training, etc.)
2. **Search viral-hooks-extracted.txt for hooks IN that domain** — Only pick hooks already from fitness/nutrition/food creators, or templates that naturally apply. Do NOT pick hooks just because structure matches.
3. **Filter for relevance to THIS specific video** — Does this hook actually make sense for THIS topic? Can I apply it naturally, or am I forcing it? Skip if forced.
4. **Filter for your avatar** — Does it speak to someone intelligent, analytical, frustrated, and plateaued?
5. **Source from proven viral outliers** — Follow the 5x rule (hooks that went viral from small-to-mid creators)
6. **Verify the video examples FIRST** — Before presenting, check: "Is this video actually relevant? Does it demonstrate what I'm claiming?"
7. **Combine across sources** — Verbal hook from one creator, written hook from another, visual from a third (variety = more original)
8. **Present 3 stacks** that feel authentically "Dar Naor" and genuinely match the video topic — science-based, direct, no fluff, content-relevant

## Key Rules for Visual Hooks (Based on Dar's Scripts)

From May & June 2026 scripts, Dar's visual hooks include:

✅ **What works:**
- Text popping on screen with specific values (grams, calories, timeframes)
- Close-ups of food with measurements
- Split screen comparisons (this vs. that)
- Shot/angle changes every 2 seconds
- Visual contrasts (black & white then color, or small vs. large)
- Overlays of graphics, checkmarks, X marks
- Quick cuts between foods/items being compared
- Meme-style faces or reactions (the "confused dog" vibe)
- Real footage of Dar in gym/kitchen doing the action

❌ **What doesn't fit:**
- Slow pans over nature
- Stock footage with no context
- Animation that distracts from message
- Overly produced graphics
- Anything that feels "fluffy" or unmotivated

## The 5x Rule Check

Every hook I present will be sourced from creators/videos that:
- Have 5x views compared to their follower count (proven viral)
- Are from small-to-mid-size creators (relatable, authentic)
- Have been posted in the last 1-2 years (fresh, trending structure)
